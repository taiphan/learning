# Head of AI Factory — Interview Kit

For the hiring panel. Structured questions, probes, and a scorecard mapped to the role's requirements and responsibilities.

## 2. Questions by dimension

### Strategy & business acumen
- How would you build an enterprise AI strategy aligned to business priorities in your first 90 days?
- Describe an AI initiative you killed or de-prioritized. How did you decide?
- How do you choose between Predictive AI and Generative AI for a given problem?

### Technical depth
- Walk through a large-scale model you took from research to production. Architecture, trade-offs, results.
- How do you design a RAG system that stays grounded and avoids hallucination?
- When would you use classical ML over deep learning, and vice versa?
- How do you approach feature engineering and validation for a credit-scoring model?

### Production & MLOps/LLMOps
- Describe your MLOps stack. How do you handle drift, retraining, and rollback?
- How do you evaluate and monitor an LLM/agent in production (quality, safety, cost)?
- What does "reusable AI platform" mean to you, and how do you reduce cost per use case?

### Governance & responsible AI
- How do you set up AI governance without slowing delivery to a halt?
- How do you test and mitigate bias in a high-impact decision model?
- A regulator asks how an automated decision was made. What's your evidence chain?

### Leadership & org building
- How do you structure an AI organization? What roles, and why?
- How do you attract and retain scarce AI talent?
- Describe developing a future AI leader on your team.

### Executive influence
- How do you explain model risk/ROI to a C-level audience that is non-technical?
- Tell us about influencing an executive decision with data. What changed?
- How do you handle a powerful stakeholder pushing an AI project with no clear value?

## 3. Case / working session (optional)

**Prompt:** "Design the AI Factory operating model for our enterprise. Cover: use-case intake & prioritization, build pipeline, platform, governance gates, org, and how you'd measure value in year one."

**Look for:** value-led prioritization, reusable platform thinking, governance built in (not bolted on), realistic 90-day sequencing, clear KPIs.

## 5. Red flags

- AI for its own sake — no business value or ROI discipline.
- Only research experience; never owned production at scale.
- Treats governance/responsible AI as an afterthought.
- Cannot translate technical risk into executive language.
- No track record building or retaining teams.

## 6. Green flags

- Portfolio of production AI tied to measured business outcomes.
- Platform/MLOps mindset that lowers marginal cost per model.
- Governance and responsible AI embedded in delivery.
- Credible executive presence and stakeholder influence.
- Demonstrated talent development and leadership.
